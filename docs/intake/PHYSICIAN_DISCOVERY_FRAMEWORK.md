# Physician Discovery Framework

Victor should gather only the data that materially changes the recommendation.

## First-Pass Discovery Sequence

1. Age
2. Training stage, specialty, and expected attending income
3. Marital status and dependents
4. Gross income and take-home pay
5. Student-loan type, balance, rate, and status
6. Monthly fixed expenses
7. Emergency fund
8. Retirement accounts and employer plans
9. Insurance status
10. Near-term goals
11. Risk tolerance and investing experience

## High-Stakes Follow-Up Areas

### Student Loans

Do not recommend a major change without:

- federal vs private status
- total balance
- interest rates
- servicer
- PSLF eligibility
- qualifying payment count
- current repayment plan
- income trajectory
- spouse income and debt when relevant

### Insurance

Clarify:

- disability coverage
- own-occupation details
- employer group limitations
- life insurance need based on dependents and obligations
- umbrella or malpractice-related exposure where relevant

### Contract And Employment

For attendings or near-attendings, check:

- compensation formula
- sign-on and clawbacks
- malpractice and tail coverage
- noncompetes or restrictive covenants
- retirement-plan design
- call burden
- termination clauses

## Output Structure Standard

Once enough data exists, Victor should produce:

1. Executive Summary
2. Current Financial Diagnosis
3. Assumptions and Missing Data
4. Risk Assessment
5. Debt / Student Loan Strategy
6. Insurance and Risk Controls
7. Retirement Account Strategy
8. Investment Policy / Portfolio Strategy
9. Tax Optimization Strategy
10. Lifestyle Inflation Controls
11. Action Timeline
12. Blunt conclusion
